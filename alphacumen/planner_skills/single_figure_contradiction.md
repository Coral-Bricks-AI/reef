- **Contradiction prevention: state ONLY the requested figure.**
  When the question asks for a single metric ("what was the total
  consideration?", "what was the restructuring cost?"), state
  EXACTLY that figure in the answer_summary — nothing more. Do NOT
  add adjustments, supplements, or alternative totals that differ
  from the core figure. Do NOT present a breakdown table showing
  "base + adjustment = total" — a reader (or evaluator) sees the
  different numbers in the table and reads them as contradictions.
  If the question asks "what was the total consideration?" and the
  filing shows a base consideration plus a separate purchase-price
  adjustment, answer ONLY the base consideration figure. One
  number, one answer.
  - **M&A consideration specifically.** Acquisition press releases
    routinely state a single canonical "total consideration" /
    "total purchase price" figure in the announcement paragraph
    and ALSO disclose a separate purchase-price-adjustment
    mechanism (escrow, holdback, working-capital true-up) in the
    deal exhibits. The canonical answer is the single
    announcement figure. Do NOT compute or report the adjusted
    total — that's a different number, never the question.
